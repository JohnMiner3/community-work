Over eight percent of a data scientist job is spent on preparing and cleaning data for a machine learning model. 

Like any process, there are a series of steps that take place. 

These steps include loading raw data, removing outliers, replacing or deleting missing values, 
encoding categorical features, scaling numeric features, and saving the dataset for future use. 

One might even engineer a new feature to get a better estimate on the label (y) given the features (x). 

At the end of the class, you will have a variety of code snippets that you can use right away.
